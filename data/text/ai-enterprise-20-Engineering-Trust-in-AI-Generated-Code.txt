Attention mechanism functions as a dynamic highlighting process. When the 
model processes “it,” it retrospectively scans approximately 110 words (~180 tokens) in 
the prompt, computing token relevance using Query–Key similarity and applying softmax 
normalization to derive context-dependent attention weights.
Complex prompts increase risk because they create:
• Competing constraints (conflicts inside the prompt)
• Long-range dependencies (early requirements get “forgotten”)
• Ambiguity (multiple valid interpretations)
• Hidden assumptions (unstated environment, libraries, policies)
What happens in the model
Drifts in probability estimation propagate into the final output, 
resulting in incorrect predictions. Thus, Security Vulnerabilities.
To be fair to models, “the model made the best guess—but it was wrong”
