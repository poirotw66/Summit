===== PAGE 5 =====
Gartner AI 
TRiSM  (AI Trust, Risk and Security Management)
The Four Pillars of AI Governance
•
Explainability & Monitoring
Action: Eliminate "Black Box" risks by ensuring AI 
decisions are auditable and understandable.
•
ModelOps  (Operational Excellence)
Action: Standardize the AI lifecycle from 
development to deployment.
•
AI Application Security
Action: Defend against specialized threats (e.g., 
Prompt Injection).
•
Privacy & Data Protection
Action: Deploy Privacy
-Enhancing Technologies 
(PETs) and Synthetic Data.
Strategic Execution Roadmap
•
Asset Rationalization Phase
Comprehensive AI Inventory: Establish an exhaustive 
catalog of all AI assets, including third
-party plugins, 
open -source models, and proprietary in
-house 
developments.
•
Governance Orchestration
Cross -Functional AI Committee: Formalize a governance 
body comprising the CIO, CISO, and Legal Counsel to 
synchronize AI initiatives with corporate risk appetite.
•
Tiered Risk Management
Contextual Classification: Implement differentiated 
TRiSM  validation standards based on use
-case risk levels 
(e.g., Internal Administrative tools vs. High
-stakes 
Customer Credit Scoring).
•
Systematic Automation
Implement 'Shift
-Left' data validation and continuous 
runtime inspection to preemptively mitigate risks, 
scaling AI trust without manual friction
